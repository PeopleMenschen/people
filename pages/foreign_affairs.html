    Here are the relevant departments of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

    Diplomatic Relations Department:

    Responsible for managing diplomatic relations with other countries, including maintaining embassies, consulates, and diplomatic missions abroad, as well as coordinating visits and exchanges between diplomats.

    International Law and Treaties Department:

    Deals with international legal matters, including treaty negotiation, interpretation, and implementation, as well as providing legal advice on international issues and disputes.

    Consular Affairs Department:

    Handles consular services for citizens traveling or residing abroad, including visa issuance, passport services, assistance to nationals in distress, and protection of citizens' rights overseas.

    Public Diplomacy and Cultural Affairs Department:

    Promotes the country's image and cultural exchange programs abroad, engages with foreign publics and media, and fosters mutual understanding and cooperation through cultural diplomacy initiatives.

    Political Affairs Department:

    Monitors political developments in other countries, analyzes foreign policies and trends, and provides policy recommendations to senior officials on political matters affecting bilateral and regional relations.

    Economic and Commercial Affairs Department:

    Promotes economic diplomacy, facilitates trade and investment opportunities, negotiates trade agreements, and supports the expansion of economic ties with foreign partners.

    Protocol and Ceremonial Affairs Department:

    Manages protocol procedures, ceremonial events, and official visits involving foreign dignitaries, as well as ensures adherence to diplomatic etiquette and protocol standards.

    The cultural system.

    The cultural system refers to the complex and interconnected network of beliefs, values, norms, symbols, traditions, customs, rituals, arts, and practices that characterize a particular society or community. It encompasses the shared meanings, identities, behaviors, and expressions that shape individuals' perceptions, interactions, and experiences within a cultural context.

    Beliefs and Values:

    Beliefs are the fundamental convictions, principles, and assumptions that individuals and groups hold about the nature of reality, existence, morality, and the world around them. Values represent the guiding principles, ideals, and standards that influence individuals' attitudes, judgments, and behaviors, shaping their priorities, choices, and aspirations within a cultural framework.

    Norms and Social Expectations:

    Norms are the unwritten rules, expectations, and standards of behavior that govern social interactions, relationships, and roles within a cultural group or community. Norms dictate acceptable and appropriate conduct in various social contexts, defining social roles, obligations, taboos, and etiquette that guide individuals' behavior and interactions with others.

    Symbols and Cultural Representations:

    Symbols are meaningful representations, signs, and expressions that convey shared meanings, values, identities, and cultural concepts within a society or group. Symbols may include language, gestures, rituals, artifacts, clothing, music, art, architecture, and religious symbols that serve as vehicles for communication, expression, and identity formation within cultural contexts.

    Traditions and Rituals:

    Traditions are long-established customs, practices, and rituals passed down from generation to generation within a cultural community. Rituals are symbolic actions, ceremonies, or rites performed to commemorate, celebrate, or enact cultural beliefs, values, milestones, and transitions, fostering social cohesion, collective memory, and continuity across time and generations.

    Language and Communication:

    Language is a fundamental component of culture, serving as the primary means of communication, expression, and cultural transmission within societies. Language encompasses verbal and nonverbal communication, dialects, accents, idioms, slang, and expressions that reflect cultural identities, worldviews, and social dynamics, facilitating socialization, collaboration, and cultural exchange.

    Arts, Literature, and Expression:

    Arts, literature, and cultural expressions encompass a diverse range of creative forms, genres, and mediums—including literature, visual arts, performing arts, music, dance, theater, film, storytelling, and folklore—that reflect and interpret cultural themes, narratives, experiences, and perspectives. Cultural expressions serve as vehicles for creativity, self-expression, identity, and cultural heritage preservation, fostering dialogue, empathy, and understanding across diverse cultural contexts.

    Cultural Institutions and Organizations:

    Cultural institutions and organizations include museums, libraries, galleries, theaters, cultural centers, educational institutions, religious institutions, and community groups that promote, preserve, and disseminate cultural heritage, knowledge, and expressions within societies. These institutions serve as hubs for cultural exchange, learning, creativity, and community engagement, fostering appreciation, diversity, and inclusivity within cultural ecosystems.

    Cultural Identity and Diversity:

    Cultural identity refers to the sense of belonging, affiliation, and attachment that individuals and groups feel toward their cultural heritage, traditions, and community. Cultural diversity encompasses the variety of cultural identities, traditions, languages, and practices present within a society or across different societies, reflecting the richness, complexity, and interconnectedness of human experiences and expressions worldwide.


    The cultural system plays a vital role in shaping individuals' identities, worldviews, relationships, and behaviors, as well as influencing societal values, institutions, and dynamics. By fostering mutual respect, understanding, and appreciation for cultural diversity, societies can promote inclusive, equitable, and harmonious interactions that celebrate the richness and plurality of human experiences and expressions across the globe.
